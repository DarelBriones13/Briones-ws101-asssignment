Rules of Taekwondo:

    1. Scoring System:
    Points are awarded for valid kicks to designated target areas, and different kicks have different point values.
    2. Matches:
    Matches are typically divided into rounds, with breaks in between. The number of rounds can vary depending on the competition.
    3. Attire:
    Competitors must wear the traditional Taekwondo uniform (dobok), and protective gear such as a helmet, chest protector, gloves, shin guards, and a groin guard.
    4. Weight Classes:
    Competitors are often divided into weight classes to ensure fair competition.
    5. Start and Stop:
    Matches begin and end with a formal bow. The referee signals the start and stop of each round.
    6. Fouls:
    Certain actions are considered fouls, such as hitting below the waist, attacking the back of the head, and excessive contact. Fouls result in penalty points or disqualification.
    7. Illegal Techniques:
    Certain techniques, like strikes with the knee or elbow, are prohibited.
    8. Points System:
    Points are awarded for kicks to specific areas of the body. High kicks to the head generally score more than kicks to the body.
    9. Warnings and Penalties:
    Competitors may receive warnings for rule infractions, and accumulating a certain number of warnings may result in point deductions or disqualification.
    10. Victory:
    A match can be won by accumulating more points than the opponent by the end of the rounds, achieving a knockout, or disqualifying the opponent.
    11. Tiebreakers:
    Certain techniques, like strikes with the knee or elbow, are prohibited.
    12. Sportsmanship:
    Respect for opponents, officials, and the spirit of Taekwondo is emphasized. Unsportsmanlike conduct can result in penalties or disqualification.
